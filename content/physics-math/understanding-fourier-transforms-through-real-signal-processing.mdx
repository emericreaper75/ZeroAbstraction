## The Language of Frequencies

At its core, the **Fourier Transform** is a mathematical tool that decomposes a complex signal into its constituent sinusoidal waves. It allows us to view a time-domain wave $x(t)$ in the frequency-domain $X(f)$.

The continuous-time Fourier transform is defined as:

$$
X(f) = int_{-infty}^{infty} x(t) e^{-i 2 pi f t} dt
$$

In practical digital signal processing, we deal with discrete, sampled data using the **Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT)**:

$$
X[k] = sum_{n=0}^{N-1} x[n] e^{-i rac{2 pi}{N} k n}
$$

### Applications in Real Engineering
- **Noise Filtering**: Easily identify high-frequency noise spikes in a sensor feed and damp them out in the frequency domain.
- **Audio Compression**: MP3 and AAC compression algorithms discard frequencies that the human ear cannot easily perceive, saving storage space.
- **Spectral Analysis**: Determine the composition of distant stars by decomposing their electromagnetic spectrum.
