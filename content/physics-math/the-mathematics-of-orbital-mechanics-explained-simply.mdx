## The Geometry of Freefall

Orbital mechanics describes the trajectories of objects moving under the influence of gravity. The foundation of modern orbital calculations relies on **Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation**:

$$
F = G rac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
$$

By combining this with Newton's second law, we derive the fundamental equation of motion for a two-body system:

$$
ddot{mathbf{r}} + mu rac{mathbf{r}}{r^3} = 0
$$

where $mu = G(m_1 + m_2)$ is the gravitational parameter.

### Conic Trajectories
Depending on the specific orbital energy $arepsilon$, trajectories trace one of three conic sections:
1. **Elliptical Orbit** ($arepsilon < 0$): Closed, periodic trajectories (e.g., planets and satellites).
2. **Parabolic Trajectory** ($arepsilon = 0$): Borderline escape trajectory.
3. **Hyperbolic Trajectory** ($arepsilon > 0$): Open escape path, often used for planetary gravity assists.
