## Democratizing the High Frontier

For decades, launching a space mission was the exclusive domain of national space agencies and multibillion-dollar telecommunication conglomerates. Standardizing satellite formats changed all of that. Enter the **CubeSat**.

A standard CubeSat unit (1U) is a cube measuring $10 	imes 10 	imes 10$ centimeters with a mass of no more than 2.0 kilograms. By standardizing these form factors, developers can leverage commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) electronics, lowering design costs by orders of magnitude.

### The Cost Revolution
- **Ride-sharing**: CubeSats launch as secondary payloads on larger rockets, sharing the massive launch costs with primary satellites.
- **COTS Components**: Instead of space-grade custom processors, developers use radiation-tolerant industrial chips, making components significantly cheaper.
- **Rapid Iteration**: Rather than spending a decade planning a single launch, researchers can design, test, and deploy a CubeSat within 1-2 years.
