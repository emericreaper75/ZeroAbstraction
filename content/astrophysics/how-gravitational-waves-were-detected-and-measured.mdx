## Measuring Spacetime Distortions

When massive cosmic objects like black holes or neutron stars collide, they generate ripples in the fabric of spacetime known as **Gravitational Waves**. Predicted by Einstein in 1916, these waves warp spacetime, stretching space in one direction and contracting it in the perpendicular axis.

### The Scale of the Challenge
By the time gravitational waves from distant collisions reach Earth, their physical displacement is incredibly small—approximately $10^{-18}$ meters (thousands of times smaller than a proton). 

To measure this, observatories like LIGO use giant **Michelson Interferometers**:
- High-power laser beams are split down two perpendicular 4-kilometer vacuum tubes.
- Light reflects off suspended mirrors and recombines at a detector.
- Spacetime distortions alter the path lengths, shifting the phase of the light waves and generating an interference pattern that reveals the cosmic event.
