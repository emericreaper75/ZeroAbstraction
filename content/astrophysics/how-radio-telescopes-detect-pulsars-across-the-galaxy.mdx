## Cosmic Lighthouses

Pulsars are highly magnetized, spinning neutron stars that emit beams of electromagnetic radiation out of their magnetic poles. As they rotate, their beams sweep across Earth, producing highly periodic, tick-like radio signals.

Because neutron stars are incredibly dense (a single teaspoon of pulsar matter weighs billions of tons on Earth), their rotational stability rivals that of atomic clocks, making them invaluable space beacons.

### The Dispersion Problem
As the pulsar's radio signals travel through the interstellar medium (ISM), free electrons delay the propagation speed. Lower frequencies travel slower than higher frequencies:

$$
Delta t propto 	ext{DM} cdot f^{-2}
$$

where $	ext{DM}$ is the **Dispersion Measure**. Radio astronomy pipelines must de-disperse incoming waveforms to align the arrival times of all frequencies before the faint periodic pulses can be detected.
