## Moving Beyond Binary

For seventy years, the dominant rule of computing was simple: digital is better. We digitized everything, enjoying absolute noise immunity and precision. However, as silicon transistors approach atomic limits, digital processors are hitting a thermal wall.

**Analog Computing** is returning as a solution. Instead of representing numbers as binary bits ($0$ and $1$), analog computers represent variables as continuously varying physical quantities like voltage or current.

### The Physics of Math
In an analog computer, mathematical operations are executed by exploiting physical laws directly:
- **Addition**: Performed instantly by combining currents at a node using Kirchhoff's Current Law.
- **Integration**: Performed by charging a capacitor through an operational amplifier.
- **Multiplication**: Accomplished using Ohm's Law ($V = I cdot R$) via adjustable resistors (memristors).

For heavy mathematical workloads like machine learning matrix multiplication, analog processors can execute calculations at a fraction of the energy required by digital GPUs.
