## Architecting for the Cosmic Void

Outside of Earth's protective magnetosphere, electronics are bombarded by high-energy cosmic rays, solar protons, and trapped radiation belts. Without shielding and specific architecture, these particles destroy digital components.

### Types of Radiation Hazards
1. **Total Ionizing Dose (TID)**: The cumulative exposure to radiation over a mission. This degrades oxide layers, eventually causing permanent leaks or chip failure.
2. **Single-Event Effects (SEE)**: Instantaneous disruptions caused by a single heavy particle.
   - **Single-Event Upsets (SEU)**: A particle flips a bit in memory.
   - **Single-Event Latchup (SEL)**: A particle triggers a short-circuit, potentially destroying the component.

### Mitigation Strategies
- **Triple Modular Redundancy (TMR)**: Implement three identical microprocessors that execute the same instructions simultaneously, using a voting circuit to select the majority result.
- **Silicon-on-Insulator (SOI)**: Building transistors on a thin layer of insulating material to prevent charge buildup and latchups.
