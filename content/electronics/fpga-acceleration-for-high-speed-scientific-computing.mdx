## Bypassing the Von Neumann Bottleneck

Standard computer systems rely on CPUs that sequentially fetch instructions from memory, execute them, and write back the results. For complex scientific simulations, this **Von Neumann Bottleneck** limits performance.

**Field-Programmable Gate Arrays (FPGAs)** offer an alternative. By configuring digital logic gates directly in hardware, developers can build custom, highly parallel compute pipelines that execute mathematical algorithms in a single clock cycle.

### Why FPGAs Win at Science
- **Massive Parallelism**: Execute thousands of mathematical operations simultaneously without resource contention.
- **Deterministic Latency**: Guarantees response times down to nanosecond precision, critical for real-time high-speed data acquisition.
- **Energy Efficiency**: Custom hardware pipelines consume significantly less power than general-purpose GPUs and CPUs.
