## Objective

This research log documents a two-week field campaign conducted to characterise the noise performance of a MEMS inertial measurement unit (IMU) mounted on a fixed-wing UAV platform. The primary objectives were:

1. Collect $\geq 20$ hours of in-flight inertial data across varying flight profiles (hover, cruise, aggressive manoeuvres)
2. Compare the in-flight noise power spectral density (PSD) against laboratory (static, vibration-isolated) characterisation
3. Identify the dominant vibration frequency bands originating from motor and propeller excitation
4. Evaluate three candidate vibration isolation mounting strategies

> **Note.** All static reference measurements were performed in the lab prior to the campaign using a 4-hour collection on a vibration-isolated granite table at $25 \pm 0.5\,°\text{C}$, following the protocol described in Research Log #001.

## Vibration Environment Analysis

The power spectral density (PSD) of the accelerometer data quantifies the vibration energy distribution across frequency. For a finite-length signal $x(t)$ of duration $T$, the PSD is defined via the periodogram:

$$
S_{xx}(f) = \lim_{T \to \infty} \frac{1}{T} \left| X_T(f) \right|^2,
$$

where $X_T(f) = \int_0^T x(t) e^{-2\pi i f t} \, dt$ is the finite-time Fourier transform. In practice, we estimate the PSD using Welch's method with 50% overlapping Hanning windows of length $2^{14} = 16384$ samples ($4.096\,\text{s}$ at $4\,\text{kHz}$), yielding a frequency resolution of $\Delta f = 0.244\,\text{Hz}$.

The dominant vibration sources and their measured PSD levels (vertical axis, $z$-accelerometer) are:

| Frequency (Hz) | Source | Accel PSD ($\text{m}^2/\text{s}^4/\text{Hz}$) |
|---|---|---|
| $78 \pm 2$ | Motor fundamental (4-pole BLDC, $\sim 2340$ RPM) | $3.2 \times 10^{-2}$ |
| $156 \pm 3$ | 2nd harmonic | $8.7 \times 10^{-3}$ |
| $234 \pm 4$ | 3rd harmonic | $2.1 \times 10^{-3}$ |
| $312 \pm 5$ | 4th harmonic (propeller blade-pass, 4-blade) | $5.6 \times 10^{-3}$ |
| $15$–$25$ | Structural resonance (frame first bending mode) | $1.8 \times 10^{-3}$ |

The motor fundamental at $78\,\text{Hz}$ dominates, contributing $>70\%$ of the total vibration energy in the $10$–$500\,\text{Hz}$ band.

## In-Flight vs Static Allan Variance Comparison

The Allan Variance (AVAR) at averaging time $\tau$ for overlapping clusters is

$$
\sigma_A^2(\tau) = \frac{1}{2\tau^2(N - 2m)} \sum_{j=1}^{N-2m} \left[\theta_{j+2m} - 2\theta_{j+m} + \theta_j\right]^2,
$$

where $m = \tau f_s$ and $\theta_j$ is the cumulative angle from integrated angular rate samples.

We extracted three noise coefficients from both the static lab dataset and the in-flight dataset (rigid mount, no vibration isolation):

| Coefficient | Static ($z$-gyro) | In-Flight ($z$-gyro) | Degradation Factor |
|---|---|---|---|
| ARW ($°/\sqrt{\text{hr}}$) | $0.0088$ | $0.028$ | $3.2\times$ |
| Bias Instability ($°/\text{hr}$) | $0.85$ | $2.4$ | $2.8\times$ |
| RRW ($°/\text{hr}^{3/2}$) | $0.12$ | $0.19$ | $1.6\times$ |

> **Warning.** The ARW degrades by $3.2\times$ in flight on the rigid mount. This exceeds the threshold for navigation-grade performance ($\text{ARW} < 0.01\,°/\sqrt{\text{hr}}$). A vibration isolation mount is mandatory for any INS/GNSS integration application requiring this sensor to operate at its static-rated noise floor.

The degradation is dominated by the motor vibration coupling into the accelerometer and gyroscope through the rigid mounting bracket. The $78\,\text{Hz}$ motor fundamental aliases into the sensor bandwidth and appears as additional broadband noise in the Allan variance at short averaging times ($\tau < 1\,\text{s}$).

## Vibration Isolation Assessment

Three isolation strategies were evaluated. Each was tested across 3 flights, with AVAR computed from 10-minute straight-and-level cruise segments to maximise stationarity.

| Mount Type | ARW Degradation (vs static) | BI Degradation | Mass (g) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Rigid aluminium bracket | $3.2\times$ | $2.8\times$ | $12$ | Baseline (reference mount) |
| Silicone rubber grommets (4×) | $2.1\times$ | $1.9\times$ | $18$ | Shore A 40 durometer, 6 mm OD |
| Foam tape (3M VHB 4910) | $1.9\times$ | $2.2\times$ | $15$ | 1 mm thickness, good damping but thermal drift |
| 3D-printed TPU mount (85A) | $1.4\times$ | $1.3\times$ | $22$ | Custom geometry, tuned to 30 Hz cutoff |

The TPU printed mount gave the best result: only $1.4\times$ ARW degradation. The design uses a four-post geometry with calculated compliance to place the mount's natural frequency at $f_n = 30\,\text{Hz}$, well below the $78\,\text{Hz}$ motor fundamental. The isolation effectiveness follows the standard transmissibility relation:

$$
T(f) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{(1 - (f/f_n)^2)^2 + (2\zeta f/f_n)^2}},
$$

where $\zeta \approx 0.15$ is the damping ratio of TPU 85A. At $f = 78\,\text{Hz}$ and $f_n = 30\,\text{Hz}$, $T \approx 0.17$ ($-15.4\,\text{dB}$), which matches the observed vibration reduction.

## Observations

1. **Motor fundamental dominance:** The $78\,\text{Hz}$ motor fundamental contributes $>70\%$ of total vibration energy. Suppressing this single frequency via notch filtering or mechanical isolation provides most of the performance recovery.

2. **Temperature sensitivity in flight:** Die temperature varied from $28\,°\text{C}$ (pre-takeoff) to $47\,°\text{C}$ (after 10 min cruise) due to self-heating and solar loading. The $19\,°\text{C}$ swing caused a bias drift of $\sim 0.4\,°/\text{hr}$ on the uncompensated $z$-gyro, consistent with the temperature coefficient $\alpha = 0.023\,°/\text{hr}/°\text{C}$ measured in Research Log #001.

3. **Propeller blade-pass frequency:** The $312\,\text{Hz}$ blade-pass frequency ($4 \times 78\,\text{Hz}$) produces a secondary PSD peak that couples into the accelerometer $x$-axis due to asymmetric propeller thrust. This has a negligible effect on gyro AVAR but introduces a $2\times 10^{-3}\,\text{m/s}^2/\sqrt{\text{Hz}}$ acceleration noise floor.

4. **Structural resonance at $18\,\text{Hz}$:** The carbon fibre frame's first bending mode at $\sim 18\,\text{Hz}$ was excited during gusts exceeding $15\,\text{km/h}$. This produced an identifiable spectral peak in all three accelerometer axes simultaneously.

5. **GPS outages:** Two flights experienced RTK fix loss for $>30\,\text{s}$ during low-bank turns. During these intervals, the IMU-only solution drifted by $0.8\,\text{m}$ (rigid mount) vs. $0.3\,\text{m}$ (TPU mount), directly demonstrating the benefit of vibration isolation for pure-inertial coasting performance.
