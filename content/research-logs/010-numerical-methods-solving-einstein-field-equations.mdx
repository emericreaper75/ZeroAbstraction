## Mathematical Challenge

Einstein's Field Equations are a system of ten highly coupled, non-linear partial differential equations. Solving them analytically is impossible for general dynamic systems like merging black holes. We must formulate them numerically.

## The ADM (3+1) Formalism

We slice 4D spacetime into a family of 3D spatial hypersurfaces parameterized by coordinate time $t$. The spacetime metric is decomposed as:

$$
ds^2 = -(alpha^2 - eta_i eta^i) dt^2 + 2 eta_i dt dx^i + gamma_{ij} dx^i dx^j
$$

where:
- $alpha$ is the lapse function, regulating coordinate time relative to proper time.
- $eta^i$ is the shift vector, describing coordinate drift along the hypersurfaces.
- $gamma_{ij}$ is the spatial 3-metric.

## Computational Results

Using a finite-difference solver with adaptive mesh refinement, we successfully simulated the metric evolution of a symmetric binary black hole system through inspiral, merger, and ringdown phases.
