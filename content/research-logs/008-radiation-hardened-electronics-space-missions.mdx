## Setup

We exposed a test chip built on a custom $180 , 	ext{nm}$ Silicon-on-Insulator (SOI) process to a $70 , 	ext{MeV}$ proton flux at the local medical cyclotron facility, simulating intense orbital radiation environments.

## Measurements and Latchup Immunity

Standard bulk silicon CMOS chips are highly vulnerable to Single-Event Latchup (SEL)—a parasitic thyristor structure is triggered by an incoming ion, short-circuiting VDD to ground. 

In our SOI design, the presence of the buried oxide (BOX) isolation layer prevents parasitic structures from forming. The test results were excellent:
- **Cumulative Dose**: Up to $150 , 	ext{krad(Si)}$ with no significant leakage current degradation.
- **SEL Events**: $0$ events recorded during a $10$-hour continuous beam exposure, confirming complete latchup immunity.
