## Experimental Design

We evaluated an entangled photon source designed for a secure quantum network. Polarization-entangled photon pairs are generated via Spontaneous Parametric Down-Conversion (SPDC) in a periodically poled potassium titanyl phosphate (ppKTP) crystal pumped by a $405 , 	ext{nm}$ laser.

## Bell Inequality Violation

To verify entanglement, we measured polarization correlation in two bases, computing the CHSH correlation parameter $S$:

$$
S = E(	heta_A, 	heta_B) - E(	heta_A, 	heta_B') + E(	heta_A', 	heta_B) + E(	heta_A', 	heta_B')
$$

Classical systems are strictly constrained to $|S| le 2$. Our experimental results yielded:

$$
S = 2.812 pm 0.015
$$

violating the classical limit by over 50 standard deviations, confirming robust quantum entanglement.
