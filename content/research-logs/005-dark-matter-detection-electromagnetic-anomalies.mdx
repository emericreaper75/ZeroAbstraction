## Experimental Setup

This log records noise characterizations within an ultra-sensitive superconducting microwave cavity ($Q approx 10^5$) suspended in an $8 , 	ext{T}$ magnetic field. The experiment searches for axions—dark matter candidates that convert to photons in magnetic fields.

## The Primakoff Effect

The interaction is governed by the axion-photon coupling Lagrangian:

$$
mathcal{L}_{agammagamma} = -g_{agammagamma} a mathbf{E} cdot mathbf{B}
$$

A dark matter axion field $a$ passing through the static magnetic field $mathbf{B}$ generates an oscillating electric field $mathbf{E}$, producing microwave photons at frequency $f = m_a c^2 / h$.

## Results

Initial sweeps between $4.5 , 	ext{GHz}$ and $4.8 , 	ext{GHz}$ showed no narrow spectral lines above the thermal noise floor ($120 , 	ext{mK}$). This constrains the coupling constant to $g_{agammagamma} < 10^{-14} , 	ext{GeV}^{-1}$ in this mass window.
