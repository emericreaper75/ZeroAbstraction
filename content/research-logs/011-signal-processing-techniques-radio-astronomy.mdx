## Spectrometer Architecture

Radio telescopes capture extremely wideband signals containing weak astronomical sources. To analyze these signals, digital backends must divide the spectrum into thousands of narrow channels. 

This log analyzes the design of an FPGA spectrometer processing $2 , 	ext{GHz}$ of instantaneous bandwidth.

## The Polyphase Filter Bank (PFB)

While a standard FFT is computationally cheap, it exhibits high spectral leakage (sidelobes). To minimize this, we implement a **Polyphase Filter Bank (PFB)**:
1. The wideband signal is split into multiple parallel sub-channels using a polyphase FIR filter.
2. An FFT is applied to the filtered outputs.

The FIR filter applies a Kaiser window to each channel, suppressing sidelobes by over $60 , 	ext{dB}$ compared to a raw FFT, preventing strong RF signals from blinding nearby astronomical observations.
