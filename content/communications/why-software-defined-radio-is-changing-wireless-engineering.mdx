## The Shift from Silicon to Software

Historically, designing radio systems required building dedicated hardware pipelines—mixers, filters, amplifiers, and modulators designed specifically for a single frequency band and modulation scheme. The hardware was rigid. If you wanted to support a new protocol, you had to spin a new board.

**Software-Defined Radio (SDR)** completely flips this paradigm. By downconverting RF signals to an intermediate frequency (IF) and digitizing them as close to the antenna as possible using high-speed ADCs/DACs, the heavy lifting of modulation, demodulation, filtering, and protocol handling is moved to general-purpose processors, FPGAs, or DSPs.

### Why It Matters
1. **Unprecedented Agility**: A single SDR transceiver can scan from a few megahertz to several gigahertz, switching protocols (e.g., from FM to LTE or custom satcom) instantly in software.
2. **Rapid Prototyping**: DSP algorithms can be developed and validated in high-level languages like Python, C++, or GNU Radio before being optimized for production hardware.
3. **Dynamic Spectrum Access**: Modern cognitive radios leverage SDR to detect unused spectrum in real-time, adapting frequencies to avoid interference dynamically.
