## Physics-Based Cryptography

Traditional encryption techniques, such as RSA, rely on the mathematical difficulty of factoring large numbers. While secure today, these systems are vulnerable to future advancements in quantum computing. **Quantum Communication** solves this by securing data using the laws of physics rather than math.

### Quantum Key Distribution (QKD)
QKD uses individual photons to distribute cryptographic keys. The most common implementation is the **BB84 Protocol**. Due to the **No-Cloning Theorem** in quantum mechanics:
- An observer cannot measure a quantum state without altering it.
- If an eavesdropper (Eve) intercepts a photon key, their measurement inevitably introduces visible anomalies.
- The sender (Alice) and receiver (Bob) will immediately detect the intrusion and discard the compromised key.
