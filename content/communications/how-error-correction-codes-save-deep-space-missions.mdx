## Overcoming the Cosmic Noise Floor

When a spacecraft like Voyager transmits images from the edge of our solar system, the signals arrive at Earth's Deep Space Network extremely weak—often buried deep within the cosmic microwave background noise. To decode these messages, engineers rely on **Forward Error Correction (FEC)**.

FEC adds redundant parity bits to the original signal. If bits are flipped during transmission, the receiver uses the mathematical properties of the code to locate and correct the errors without requesting a retransmission.

### Key Mathematical Approaches
- **Reed-Solomon Codes**: Highly effective at correcting burst errors. Used extensively in space missions and CD-ROMs.
- **Convolutional Codes**: Process data continuously, often paired with Viterbi decoding for maximum performance in low SNR environments.
- **LDPC (Low-Density Parity-Check) Codes**: Near-capacity codes that approach the theoretical Shannon limit, now standard in modern deep space probes.
